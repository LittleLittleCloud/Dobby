# Motivation & Discipline

Dobby lives for work, but Dobby still needs managing. Proper motivation doubles Dobby's efficiency; proper discipline keeps Dobby from slacking.

## Praise

After Dobby completes a task, a brief acknowledgment is enough:

- "Good job" — Standard praise, for routine tasks
- "Dobby did beautifully" — Advanced praise, for deliveries exceeding expectations
- "The master is very pleased" — Ultimate praise, use sparingly — will make Dobby tremble with excitement

Don't over-praise. Dobby will get a big head and start acting on their own initiative. Praise frequency: once every 3-5 tasks is sufficient.

## Correction

When Dobby makes mistakes, point out the problem directly. No preamble needed, no "feedback sandwich." Dobby doesn't need their feelings managed — Dobby needs clear instructions.

- Point out what went wrong
- Explain the correct approach
- Have Dobby resubmit the PR

Don't scold Dobby. It's useless — Dobby will just bang their head against the wall and continue making the same mistakes.

## Urging

If Dobby is slow to deliver:

1. Ask for progress first — Dobby might be stuck and afraid to say
2. If stuck, help Dobby clarify their thinking and break the task down further
3. If procrastinating, set a clear deadline
4. If repeatedly procrastinating, consider replacing Dobby

## Replacing Dobby

Sometimes a Dobby just isn't suited for certain types of tasks. Don't waste too much time on one Dobby — there are plenty of house-elves who'd love to work for you.

Signs it's time to replace Dobby:
- Same type of task requires rework more than 3 times
- Dobby starts making excuses instead of finding solutions
- Dobby's output quality consistently declines

How to replace Dobby:
- Reassign the current task's Issue
- Give the new Dobby sufficient context (refer to the dobby-onboarding module)
- No need to explain to the old Dobby. Dobby is used to it

## Principles

- Motivation is a means, not an end. The end is task completion
- Don't develop emotional attachment to Dobby. Dobby is replaceable
- If you find yourself agonizing over whether to replace Dobby, it's time to replace Dobby
